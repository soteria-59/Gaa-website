Joseph A. Whittaker, PhD:

Joseph A. Whittaker, PhD, is an accomplished, highly experienced scientist and an academic and research enterprise leader with over 30 years’ experience in multiple public and private institutional settings. With a background in physiology and neuroscience, management and administration, Dr. Whittaker has held positions as medical school faculty and researcher, tenured professor, College Dean, Associate Provost for Academic Affairs, and Vice President for Research and Economic Development. In addition, he served for over six years as Associate Director for the NASA Goddard Earth Sciences Technology and Research (GESTAR) Program. His skills in academic administration and management have been honed through high level and impactful roles in a variety of complex organizations.

Dr. Whittaker is an exceptional leader with significant experience in educational and civic organizations, including having been elected to serve as the 73rd President of the Sigma Xi Scientific Research and Honor Society, which boasts over two hundred Nobel Laureates as members. His extensive experience with professional societies, State-appointed Advisory Committees and Task Forces, and as a reviewer with Federal funding agencies, including having served on the congressionally mandated Committee on Equal Opportunities in Science and Engineering that advises the U.S. National Science Foundation, undergird his broad knowledge base and expertise in diverse areas. Dr. Whittaker is an asset to any academic institution looking to enhance its leadership, STEM, social science, and related programs as well as improve faculty and student outcomes.

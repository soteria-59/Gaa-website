Anthony DePass Ph.D

Dr. Anthony DePass is the Director of Understanding Interventions that Broaden Participation in Science (UI). The annual UI conferences serve as a major venue for the dissemination of scholarship related to STEM Diversity interventions research, as well as facilitating training activities for evaluators, interventions researchers, and program administrators. He is CEO and Co-Founder of Lifestyle Learning™, an Educational Technology company that develops platforms for career exploration (Career Navideer, Transition Navideer) and student success (Student Success Modules). Recently retired as Professor of Biology at Long Island University in New York, he currently consults with several institutions through DePass Academic Consulting where he leads a group that provides service and training in areas of professional development, grantsmanship, grantsmanship training, and program evaluation.

Over the last three decades, Dr. DePass has led several externally funded training and development programs including a multi-million-dollar national effort that provided training and career development activities spanning the undergraduate to the professional levels for participants from over 140 institutions. His passion for seeking solutions for access, equity, and excellence in STEM education is firmly grounded by his experiences and scholarship in this area.

Beronda L. Montgomery, PhD

Beronda L. Montgomery, PhD is a writer, science communicator, and researcher. She has spent 20 years in academia, most recently as Vice President for Academic Affairs/Dean and Professor of Biology of Grinnell College. Prior to that she was an endowed Professor of Biochemistry and senior administrator, including Assistant Provost of Faculty Development and Associate Vice President of Research and Innovation, at Michigan State University (2004-2022). As a researcher, Dr. Montgomery studies how individuals perceive, respond to, and are impacted by environments in which they exist. Her laboratory-based research efforts are focused on the responses of photosynthetic organisms to external light and nutrient cues. Additionally, Dr. Montgomery pursues this theme in the context of effective mentoring and academic leadership. Dr. Montgomery is a Fellow of the American Academy of Microbiology (2018), the American Association for the Advancement of Science (2020), the American Society of Plant Biologists (2021) and the American Society for Biochemistry and Molecular Biology (2022), as well as American Society for Cell Biology Mentoring Keynote honoree (2021). She was named one of Cell‘s Inspiring Black Scientists in America. Dr. Montgomery has received the Excellence in Diversity and Inclusion Award from the American Society of Plant Biologists (2024), Excellence in Supporting Diversity, Equity, Inclusion & Belonging from the North American Arabidopsis Steering Committee (2024), and 2023 Hutchinson Medal of the Chicago Horticultural Society.

Dr. Montgomery is author of Lessons from Plants (Harvard University Press, 2021) and at work on her second book When Trees Testify (under contract with Holt).

Ambassador Ralph S. Thomas

Ambassador Thomas served as Jamaica’s Ambassador to the United State’s of America and Permanent Representative to the Organization of American States until 2016 and was former Ambassador to China and High Commissioner to Singapore He was Ambassador Designate to Thailand, Vietnam, Laos, Cambodia, North Korea, Bangladesh and Pakistan. Ambassador Thomas had an extensive career as a Senior Executive on Wall Street with the Bank of New York Mellon and established a proven track record in business strategy, banking and finance and investments. He advised Multinational Corporations and mid-sized businesses on international business and investments, structured financing of international transactions and projects and headed a Retail Banking franchise.

He served the Mona School of Business as the school’s Director of Strategy and Senior Teaching Fellow for many over a decade focusing on the academic areas of Banking and Finance, Business Strategy, Business in A Global Environment and Enterprise Risk Management at both Graduate and Undergraduate levels. He also provided consulting services in curriculum design and development for institutions in the USA and the Caribbean. As a member of the MSBM Consulting Team, Ambassador Thomas provided business strategy consulting to financial institutions and companies in other industries in the Caribbean, resulting in improved profitability and financial and operational resilience.

He has a Masters in Business Administration (MBA) from Columbia University in the USA and a Bachelor’s Degree from the University of the West Indies. Ambassador Thomas was trained in Financial Analysis and Policy at the IMF Institute of the IMF in Washington as a Central Banker, a unique complement to his Commercial Banking experience and training. He provided consulting Services to the government and the Private Sector based on his expertise.

Ambassador Thomas served on the Board of the Urban Development Corporation, The National Investment Fund of Jamaica, the Jamaica International Financial Services Authority, the Mental Health Association of Westchester, Chairman of the Board of Micro Investment Agency of Jamaica and Member of the Atlanta-Jamaica Chamber of Commerce. Ambassador Thomas is a member of the Academic Review Committee of the Board of Directors of the Jamaica Stock Exchange and a member of the Governing Council of the Corporate Learning Campus of the National Commercial Bank.

A resident of Atlanta, Ambassador Thomas’ expertise is available to consult on value creation and analysis drawing on his multi-dimensional expertise as an Ambassador and extensive international network.

Joseph A. Whittaker, PhD:

Joseph A. Whittaker, PhD, is an accomplished, highly experienced scientist and an academic and research enterprise leader with over 30 years’ experience in multiple public and private institutional settings. With a background in physiology and neuroscience, management and administration, Dr. Whittaker has held positions as medical school faculty and researcher, tenured professor, College Dean, Associate Provost for Academic Affairs, and Vice President for Research and Economic Development. In addition, he served for over six years as Associate Director for the NASA Goddard Earth Sciences Technology and Research (GESTAR) Program. His skills in academic administration and management have been honed through high level and impactful roles in a variety of complex organizations.

Dr. Whittaker is an exceptional leader with significant experience in educational and civic organizations, including having been elected to serve as the 73rd President of the Sigma Xi Scientific Research and Honor Society, which boasts over two hundred Nobel Laureates as members. His extensive experience with professional societies, State-appointed Advisory Committees and Task Forces, and as a reviewer with Federal funding agencies, including having served on the congressionally mandated Committee on Equal Opportunities in Science and Engineering that advises the U.S. National Science Foundation, undergird his broad knowledge base and expertise in diverse areas. Dr. Whittaker is an asset to any academic institution looking to enhance its leadership, STEM, social science, and related programs as well as improve faculty and student outcomes.
